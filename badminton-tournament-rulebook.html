01

Reporting & Check-in

  • Report to the registration desk at least 30 minutes before your first scheduled match.
  • Stay available throughout the tournament until you are eliminated.
  • Once a match is announced, report to the assigned court immediately.
  • Failing to report within 10 minutes of the scheduled match time may result in a walkover.
02

Match Format

  • The tournament follows a knockout format.
  • All matches before the finals are a single game to 21 points.
  • The finals are best of three games to 15 points (or another format announced before play if time is constrained).
  • The Tournament Committee may modify the match format if required by time constraints or unforeseen circumstances.
03

Scoring System

Golden point. At 20–20, the side that wins the next rally wins the game (game point at 21).
04

Warm-up

  • A maximum of 2 minutes is allowed for on-court warm-up.
  • Complete your stretching and practice outside the playing courts before your match.
05

Shuttle Policy

  • Yonex Mavis 350 nylon shuttlecocks are used throughout the tournament.
  • Tournament shuttles are provided only by the organizers.
  • Players may not replace tournament shuttles with personal shuttlecocks during official matches.
  • The court marshal decides when a shuttle needs to be replaced.
06

Equipment & Dress Code

  • Indoor non-marking badminton shoes are mandatory.
  • Players bring their own racquets and other personal equipment.
07

Court Allocation

  • Court assignments are made solely by the Tournament Committee.
  • Players may not request a change of court.
  • Organizers may shift matches between courts whenever necessary.
08

Walkovers

A walkover may be awarded if a player or team:

  • Fails to report within the permitted time (10 minutes of announcement).
  • Withdraws from the match.
  • Refuses to play.
  • Is disqualified by the Tournament Committee.
09

Line Calls & Disputes

  • In a disagreement, the court marshal or tournament official makes the final decision.
  • Unsportsmanlike arguments or repeated disputes may result in disqualification.
  • Call line decisions honestly and treat opponents, volunteers, and organizers with respect. Any attempt to gain an unfair advantage through deception may result in disqualification.
10

Medical & Injury

  • Players may request a medical timeout in case of injury.
  • Medical timeouts are granted only for genuine injuries and generally will not exceed five minutes.
  • If a player cannot continue after the allotted time, the opponent is declared the winner.
11

Player Conduct

Players are expected to maintain the highest standards of sportsmanship. The following is strictly prohibited:

  • Abusive or offensive language.
  • Throwing racquets or damaging equipment.
  • Deliberately delaying matches.
  • Intimidating opponents or officials.
  • Physical or verbal altercations.
  • Any form of cheating or misrepresentation.

Violations may escalate

Verbal warning Point penalty Match forfeiture Disqualification Removal from venue
12

Prize Distribution

Winner ₹5,000 + Trophy
Runner-up ₹2,000 + Trophy

Prize distribution takes place immediately after the completion of the finals.

13

Tournament Schedule

  • The schedule is published before the tournament, available via QR code and at the Tournament Desk.
  • Players are responsible for checking the schedule regularly.
  • The Tournament Committee may revise the schedule if required.
14

Refreshments

  • Bananas will be available on site.
  • Refreshments are complimentary and distributed once per registered participant.
15

Liability

Participation is entirely at the player's own risk. The organizers are not responsible for:

  • Personal injuries.
  • Loss of personal belongings.
  • Damage to personal equipment or vehicles.
  • Any unforeseen incidents beyond their control.
16

Tournament Committee

The Tournament Committee reserves the right to:

  • Interpret these rules.
  • Modify the match schedule.
  • Merge or cancel categories.
  • Resolve disputes.
  • Take decisions in situations not covered by this rulebook.

All decisions of the Tournament Committee are final and binding.

Good luck on court.
